Module 1: Bonjou! (Greetings & Introductions)

Learn essential greetings and family introductions in Martinican Creole

Lesson 1.1: Basic Greetings

Vocabulary

Bonjou - Good morning/Hello

Bonso - Good evening

Kouman ou yé? - How are you?

M'ap boule - I'm fine (literally "I'm burning")

Mèsi - Thank you

Orevwa - Goodbye

Pronunciation Guide

Bonjou: [bon-zhoo]

Kouman ou yé?: [koo-man oo yay]

M'ap boule: [m-ap bool]

Mèsi: [meh-see]

Orevwa: [oh-reh-vwah]

Cultural Context

In Martinican culture, greetings are extremely important and often more elaborate than in Western cultures. It's customary to greet everyone individually when entering a room, and to ask about their well-being and family. The expression "M'ap boule" (I'm burning) reflects the vibrant, energetic spirit of Martinican people - it conveys that you're doing well and full of life!

When meeting someone for the first time, it's common to shake hands firmly while maintaining eye contact. Among friends and family, cheek kisses (usually two or three) are common. Always use formal greetings with elders and in professional settings.
